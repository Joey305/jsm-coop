**The Man in the Ball Cap** has taken a new step on the Camino.

We are excited to share that the novel is now available in Spanish — opening the story to more readers, more communities, and more people who can walk with JSM Cooperative in its mission to turn storytelling into impact.

This is more than a translation.

It is an invitation.

An invitation for Spanish-speaking readers to enter the mystery, follow the story, and become part of the larger Camino behind JSM Cooperative Corporation.

---

## A story crossing languages

At **JSM Cooperative Corporation**, we believe that stories should not stay locked inside one place, one language, or one audience.

Stories are meant to travel.

They are meant to move through families, communities, friendships, cities, and cultures.

The Spanish edition of *The Man in the Ball Cap* allows the story to reach readers who may connect with it in a deeper, more personal way — especially in places like **A Coruña, Galicia**, where so much of the emotional spirit behind JSM Cooperative began to take shape.

Galicia gave us more than scenery.

It gave us a feeling.

A sense of movement.

A sense of memory.

A sense of walking toward something meaningful.

That feeling lives inside the Camino.

And now, through the Spanish edition, more readers can join that path.

## Why Spanish matters

Spanish is one of the most widely spoken languages in the world, but for JSM Cooperative, this decision is not only about reach.

It is about belonging.

The Camino is supposed to be a path for many people. It should welcome readers from different places, different backgrounds, and different languages.

By offering *The Man in the Ball Cap* in Spanish, we are making the story more accessible to readers who may see themselves, their communities, or their landscapes reflected in the emotional world of the book.

This is especially meaningful because the spirit of the Camino is deeply connected to Galicia — to its coast, its rain, its old streets, its quiet mystery, and its sense that every path carries memory.

---

## A tale of two languages

The Spanish edition keeps the heart of the original story while opening it to a new audience.

The mystery remains.

The atmosphere remains.

The emotional weight remains.

But the language changes the way the story breathes.

That is the beauty of translation. A story can remain itself while becoming new again.

For JSM Cooperative, that matters because our work is not just about publishing books. It is about building bridges through creative work — bridges between readers, causes, communities, and people who might otherwise never meet.
