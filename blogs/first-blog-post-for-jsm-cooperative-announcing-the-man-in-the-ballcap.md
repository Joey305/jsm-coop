JSM Cooperative Corporation is proud to announce **The Man in the Ball Cap**.

This debut novel marks the beginning of an important chapter for the Co-op: a story rooted in mystery, atmosphere, and purpose — and the first major creative work connected to our larger mission.

At JSM Cooperative, we believe books can do more than entertain.

They can gather community.

They can support meaningful causes.

They can become part of a shared path.

They can become a Camino.

---

## A mystery begins in A Coruña

**The Man in the Ball Cap** takes readers into the vibrant and shadowed streets of **A Coruña, Galicia**.

The story follows **Pepe Miguel**, a private investigator pulled into a world of secrets, symbols, and unanswered questions.

Ancient atmosphere meets modern mystery.

The coastline, the stone streets, the Atlantic air, and the history of the city all become part of the story’s emotional landscape.

A Coruña is not just where the novel happens.

It is part of the mystery.

---

## More than a detective story

At its surface, **The Man in the Ball Cap** is a mystery.

But beneath the clues, the tension, and the investigation, the book also represents something larger for JSM Cooperative.

It is the first step in building a creative-impact model around storytelling.

The question behind the launch was simple:

> Can a novel become more than a story?

For us, the answer was yes.

A novel can become a bridge between readers and causes.

A novel can bring people into a mission.

A novel can help launch a community.

## A story with a purpose

JSM Cooperative was created to connect creative work with mission-driven impact.

That means **The Man in the Ball Cap** is not only a book launch. It is part of a wider effort to use stories, campaigns, newsletters, donations, and community support to help meaningful work grow.

One of the earliest connections behind this mission is **Mindful Diabetes Inc.**, whose work focuses on education and awareness around diabetes, metabolic health, Alzheimer’s risk, and the idea often discussed as Type 3 diabetes.

This connection matters because stories can open doors to difficult conversations.

They can help people care.

They can make complex topics feel human.

They can turn awareness into action.
